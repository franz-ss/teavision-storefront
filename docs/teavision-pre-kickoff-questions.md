# Pre-Kickoff Conversations — Question Sets

Three conversations to have before the rebuild kicks off. Each is sized for 15–20 minutes. The goal is to surface the things that would change the project plan, not to do exhaustive discovery — admin access and the audit handle most of the technical fact-finding.

Each conversation is structured so the most consequential questions come first. If time runs short, the early questions are the ones that matter.

---

## 1. Business Owner / Decision-Maker

**Purpose:** confirm the rebuild scope reflects actual business priorities, especially around the retail/wholesale split that the audit surfaced.

### Revenue and conversion paths

1. Roughly what proportion of revenue comes from retail website orders versus wholesale accounts versus custom blend / private label / manufacturing services? A rough split is fine — 70/20/10, half-half, whatever the shape is.

2. Of the inquiries that come in through the website (wholesale account applications, custom blend requests, catalogue downloads, contact forms), which ones actually convert to revenue, and how often? Which ones are noise?

3. Where do new wholesale customers typically discover Teavision? Search, referral, trade events, outbound, something else?

### The wholesale flow

4. Walk me through what happens after someone clicks "Apply Now" on the wholesale account page. Who reads it, how fast, what's the follow-up? Where does it currently fall short?

5. Same question for custom blend inquiries and private label inquiries — what's the journey from form submission to first order?

6. Is there anything about the wholesale customer experience on the current site that's actively costing you business? Things buyers complain about, things sales has to work around, anything you wish the site did but doesn't.

### Priorities for the rebuild

7. If the rebuild only meaningfully improved one thing for the business, what would you most want it to be? Not the answer you'd give in a press release — the honest answer.

8. Are there features or capabilities you've wanted to add that the current Shopify theme has been blocking? Anything specific your team has had to say "no" to or work around because of platform limits?

9. Is there a target launch date driven by anything specific — a campaign, a trade event, a fiscal milestone, a contract — or is "as soon as it's ready" the timeline?

### The domain question

10. The footer login link points to `mrtea.com.au` while the main site is `teavision.com.au`. What's the story there — old brand, separate store, just a stale link? Is `mrtea.com.au` still actively used for anything?

### The previous theme work

11. The original brief mentioned a new theme being customised by someone else. What's the status of that work — finished, abandoned, ongoing? Should I be coordinating with anyone, or is it fully closed out?

### Open question

12. Anything I haven't asked that you think I should know before scoping this properly?

---

## 2. SEO Owner

**Purpose:** understand what's currently working in SEO terms, what the strategy is, and what absolutely cannot break in the rebuild. This person becomes a project partner from this point onward, not a Phase 5 consultant.

### The current SEO picture

1. What's driving the bulk of organic traffic right now — is it product pages, the location-based wholesale pages (Perth, Adelaide, etc.), the blog, the content pages like "Global Tea Suppliers" and "Tea Importers Australia," or something else?

2. Which keywords or query types matter most commercially? "Wholesale tea Australia," location-based terms, specific product searches, or B2B / category terms?

3. Are there pages that are currently over-performing — ranking better than you'd expect — that we need to be especially careful with in the migration?

4. Are there pages currently underperforming that we should treat as opportunities to improve in the rebuild rather than just preserve?

### The strategy behind what's on the site

5. The footer has ~100 keyword-targeted links to collections. What's the thinking there — is this active link-equity distribution, historical accumulation, or both? How important is preserving this exact structure?

6. The location-based wholesale collections (`/collections/tea-wholesale-perth`, etc.) — are these driving real traffic and conversions, or are they legacy pages? Which cities have these, and is the list growing?

7. The content pages targeting commercial intent — "Global Tea Suppliers," "Tea Importers Australia," "Custom Tea Blends," etc. — are these earning their keep? Should the rebuild preserve, expand, or rationalize them?

8. The Tea Journal blog publishes regularly. What's the editorial strategy — keyword-targeted topics, expertise-building, brand awareness, all of the above? Who writes it?

### Tag-based and filtered collections

9. The site has tag-based collection URLs like `/collections/all/categories_all-herbs` and filter combinations. Some of these may be indexed. Do you have visibility into whether they're driving traffic, or are they incidental?

10. How are filtered collection URLs handled today — canonicalized to the unfiltered collection, or treated as separate pages?

### What you'd want from the rebuild

11. If you had a dream list of SEO improvements the rebuild could deliver, what would be on it? Things you've wanted to fix but the current theme has made hard.

12. Conversely, what's working well now that you're worried about losing in the migration? The "please don't touch this" list.

### Working together through the rebuild

13. What's the level of involvement that works for you during the project — review at key checkpoints, weekly sync, full integration into the team? Whatever it is, I'd rather know now than figure it out later.

14. Do you have access to Search Console, GA4, and any rank-tracking tools? Can I get read access for the project, or would you rather pull reports?

15. Are there other vendors involved in the SEO work — link building, content writing, technical consultants — that I should be aware of and possibly coordinate with?

### Risk and rollback

16. If the rebuild caused a 20% organic traffic drop in the first month post-launch, how serious would that be for the business, and what's our agreed response? Roll back, push through, something in between? Worth aligning on this before we need it, not during a crisis.

## 3. Operations / Customer Service

**Purpose:** surface the operational reality of how the storefront actually serves customers and the team. This conversation often reveals scope items that nobody else thinks to mention.

### Wholesale and inquiry flows

1. When someone applies for a wholesale account through the website, what happens on your side? Where does the application land, who handles it, and what's the typical timeline?

2. Same for custom blend inquiries, private label inquiries, and the new product development form — who owns each, and what does the back-office workflow look like?

3. Are there inquiries that get lost or fall through the cracks today? Is there a notification, CRM, or email routing setup that's working well, or one that's frustrating?

### Customer-facing problems

4. What are the most common questions or complaints customer service handles about the website specifically? Things people can't find, can't do, or get confused by.

5. Are there things customers regularly ask for that the current site doesn't support — that you have to handle manually?

6. Anything broken on the current site that you've had to work around, even if it's been broken so long it feels normal?

### Wholesale customer reality

7. How do existing wholesale customers reorder — through the website, by emailing or calling sales, through a separate portal, or some mix? If they use the website, what's their experience like compared to a retail buyer?

8. Do wholesale customers see different prices, different product availability, or different content than retail visitors? If so, how is that controlled today?

9. Is there an account-level pricing or quoting system, or does each wholesale order get manually priced?

### The app stack from an operational view

10. Of the apps installed on the store — reviews, popups, forms, subscription tools, shipping management — which ones do you and the team actually use day-to-day? Which ones are installed but no one touches?

11. Are there any apps that, if they disappeared tomorrow, would cause real operational pain? And conversely, any that you'd be quietly relieved to retire?

12. The site has multiple form-builder apps installed (Powerful Form Builder, Zotabox Contact Form Builder, Shopify Forms). Do you know which one is actually being used for which forms?

### Team workflows

13. Who currently updates the website — adds products, edits content, runs promotions, publishes blog posts? How often, and how much pain is involved?

14. Are there things the team needs to do regularly that the current site makes hard or slow? Be specific if you can — "updating the homepage hero" or "adding a new collection" rather than "everything is annoying."

15. After the rebuild ships, who do you imagine will be making routine updates — internal team, agency, me, a mix? This affects how I build the content management side of things.

### Customer support during the launch

16. During the launch window, what kind of customer-facing issues are you most worried about? What would make your job during launch week easier?

17. Are there scripted responses or FAQs you'll need updated for the new site, and how much lead time do you need to prepare?

### Open question

18. Is there anything operational about the business that I should know about that probably won't come up in any other conversation? Quirks, exceptions, edge cases, the things that "everyone just knows."

---

## How to use these

**These are starting points, not scripts.** Read the questions, then have a real conversation. Skip questions that get answered along the way. Follow up on things that surprise you — those are usually the most valuable parts of the conversation.

**One conversation at a time.** Don't try to combine the three. The business owner conversation should happen first because it sets context for the others. The SEO owner conversation should happen before any redirect or URL strategy work. Operations can come last and benefits from already understanding the business priorities.

**Ask permission to record or take notes openly.** Especially for the SEO owner — there's enough technical detail in those answers that note-taking matters.

**The questions you don't ask matter too.** None of these conversations should feel like an interrogation. If 12 questions takes 30 minutes, that's a sign the conversation went somewhere useful and you should let it.

**Capture decisions, not just answers.** After each conversation, write a short note: what was decided, what's still open, what changed about the project plan. These notes become the source of truth when something gets contested in week 6.
