# Draft Message — Pre-Kickoff Conversation Request

A draft message to send to the business owner / decision-maker to surface the audit findings and request the conversation before kickoff. Tone is collaborative and direct, not pitchy. The goal is to get a 20-minute slot on the calendar with enough context that the conversation is productive when it happens.

Three versions below at decreasing length, depending on the relationship and how much context the recipient prefers up front. Pick whichever feels right.

---

## Version A — Full context (email-length)

**Subject:** Quick read on the storefront rebuild — a few things worth talking through before we start

Hi [name],

I've been doing the audit work for the storefront rebuild and wanted to surface a few things before we kick off properly. Three findings shifted my thinking on the project, and I'd like 20 minutes to walk through them with you so we scope the right thing.

**1. The site is more B2B than the brief described.** The homepage leads with the wholesale account application, the testimonials are all from business buyers, and the product pages explicitly invite buyers to ask for samples and wholesale pricing. The retail Shopify checkout works, but the conversion paths that actually drive revenue look like wholesale applications, custom blend inquiries, catalogue downloads, and direct phone contact. I want to make sure the rebuild reflects that reality rather than treating it as a retail storefront with B2B on the side.

**2. The SEO investment is substantial and very visible.** The footer keyword links, the location-based wholesale pages (Perth, Adelaide, etc.), the content pages targeting B2B intent, the active blog — there's a lot of deliberate work here, and it's protecting real organic traffic. I'd like to identify whoever owns SEO and bring them into the project from now, not from the launch phase. Without that, the migration carries more risk than it should.

**3. There's a domain question.** The footer login link points to `mrtea.com.au` while the rest of the site is `teavision.com.au`. Probably a stale link or a domain shift that wasn't fully cleaned up, but I want to confirm before we touch anything.

None of these are problems with the project — they're things I want to understand before scoping the work properly. The rebuild is still the right move, but the audit changed how I'd approach a few pieces of it.

Could we grab 20 minutes this week? I have a short list of questions — nothing exhaustive, just the things that need your input rather than mine. Happy to send them ahead if that's useful, or we can just talk it through.

Thanks,
[your name]

## Notes on which version to use

**Version A** — for relationships where written context is the norm and the recipient prefers reading over meeting. Email-appropriate. Useful if the recipient is the type who wants to think before talking.

**Version B** — the safe default. Long enough to land the three findings substantively, short enough that the recipient won't put off reading it. Works in either email or Slack.

**Version C** — for high-trust, fast-moving relationships where you'd normally just grab a few minutes. The findings get hinted at; the conversation does the work. Risk: the three findings might land harder in the meeting than the message implies.

## Notes on framing

A few things were deliberate in all three versions:

- **"Shifted my thinking" rather than "found problems"** — the findings aren't problems with the business, they're updates to my model. Framing them as my recalibration rather than as issues with the site keeps the tone collaborative.

- **"The rebuild is still the right move"** — said explicitly, because the findings could otherwise read as "should we even do this." Removing that ambiguity protects the project and the relationship.

- **"Things that need your input rather than mine"** — signals the meeting is short and focused, not a generic discovery session. Busy people respond better to specific asks.

- **No mention of pricing, scope changes, or contract.** The conversation is about getting the work right; commercial implications can come later if the conversation surfaces real scope changes.

- **"20 minutes" specifically.** Names the time commitment so they can decide quickly. "A quick chat" reads as open-ended and gets deferred.

## What to send with the message

If you have the question set ready, attaching or linking the business-owner question list is fine for Versions A and B. For Version C, save the questions for the meeting itself — sending a 12-question list with a one-paragraph message reads inconsistent.

Don't send the full audit document with the initial message. The audit findings will be more useful as a follow-up after the conversation, when you're aligning on the revised plan. Sending it up front buries the lede and makes the conversation reactive rather than directional.
