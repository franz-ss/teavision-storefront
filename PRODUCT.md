# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** B2B purchasing managers at cafes, restaurants, retailers, and wellness brands. Time-pressured, goal-oriented, scanning for supplier credibility fast: certifications, MOQs, pricing tiers, lead times. Trust is the purchase. They need to hand this URL to a colleague or boss and have it close itself.

**Secondary:** Retail consumers researching premium loose-leaf tea. Discovery-oriented, motivated by origin stories and health properties, likely to start with a small sample before scaling.

## Product Purpose

Teavision is Australia's largest wholesale tea, herb, and spice supplier — 1,000+ ingredients including 500+ certified organic, sourced from 15+ countries, serving 1,000+ businesses. The headless storefront exists to replace a constrained Shopify Liquid theme with a design system that can move fast, rank well, and convert both B2B inquiries and retail samples.

Primary revenue drivers: wholesale account applications, custom blend and private-label inquiries, catalogue downloads with lead capture, retail sample sales. Add-to-cart and wholesale apply are equally important actions.

## Brand Personality

Heritage authority that wears it lightly. 15+ industry awards, HACCP and ACO certified, established 2014 — this is the category leader, not an upstart. The tone is warm and specific, not corporate: "quintessential black tea," real farm origins, real testimonials from St. Ali and Remedy Drinks. Confident without being stiff.

Three words: **authoritative, warm, precise.**

## Anti-references

- Artisan craft-tea hipster aesthetic: small-batch single-origin, hand-stamped kraft paper, overworked texture. Teavision is a serious supplier at scale; it should not look like a farmers' market.
- Clinical wellness white: lab-coat health brand with mint accents and floating molecule graphics. This is food, not pharma.
- Mass-market grocery commodity: Lipton/Tetley brand language, primary-color banner ads, coupon energy.
- Generic B2B SaaS: pricing tables, feature grids, testimonial carousels with headshots and star ratings. This is a product business, not a subscription tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Authority through specificity.** Real numbers, named origins, certification logos, named clients. Vague claims ("premium quality") disappear; specific ones ("ACO Organic, sourced from 15 countries, serving St. Ali") build trust.
2. **B2B conversion is the primary goal.** Wholesale inquiry and account application CTAs must be prominent and frictionless on every relevant surface. They are never secondary to retail add-to-cart.
3. **The product carries the brand.** Photography and ingredient variety are the strongest visual assets. Design supports, never competes. Let scale and range speak.
4. **Warm professionalism.** The brand has the authority to be confident; it also serves people who make tea for a living. Keep the tone human. Avoid stiffness, but never be casual.
5. **SEO is structural.** 100+ keyword-targeted footer links, location-based wholesale pages, and an active blog are not decoration. They are traffic infrastructure that must survive the migration intact.
